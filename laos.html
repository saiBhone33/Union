Laos


Laos, officially the Lao People's Democratic Republic (LPDR), is the only landlocked country in Southeast Asia. It consists of an irregularly round portion in the north that narrows into a peninsula-like region stretching to the southeast.

Weather

Lao has a tropical climate, which is influenced by the southeast monsoon which causes significant rainfall and high humidity. The climate is divided into two distinct seasons: rainy season, from May to mid-October, followed by a dry season from mid-October to April. Average temperatures in the northern and eastern mountainous areas and the plateaus are 20°C , and in the plains 25-27°C.

Economy


The main sector of Lao economy is agriculture, which contributes around 51% of the country's GDP and employs over 80% of the labor force. However, the country's economy is also driven by trade, investment, tourism, mining and hydropower.

Ethnic Groups

Laos is considered the most ethnically diverse country in Southeast Asia. The country's ethnic groups are categorized into four ethno-linguistic families with Lao-Tai making up 62.4%, Mon-Khmer making up 23.7%, Hmong-Iu Mien making up 9.7%, Chine-Tibetan making up 2.9% and other ethnic groups making up 1.3% of the population.

The official language of Laos is Lao and it is used by 52% of the national population. English and French are the foreign languages that a small percentage of Laos can speak. Laos boasts a mosaic of languages spoken by its diverse minority ethnic groups, in which the most common are Khmu and Hmong languages.

Famous Festival

Boun Pi May or Lao New Year Festival is one of the most important holidays in Laos when everyone gathers, pray for happiness, welcome the New Year, especially the amazing moments of water splash. The festival’s highlight is the throwing of water as Laotians believe that the old spirits will be washed away and welcome the New Year.

Traditional clothing

In Laos, women traditionally wear a sinh, which is a handmade, silk skirt with a suea pat, a long-sleeved shirt which is wrapped around the body. The traditional dress for women in Laos is finished off with a pha biang, a thin garment which is women over one shoulder.

The traditional costume for men is called a xout lao which is hip-length tailored jacket, known as a Nehru jacket, made of white silk. This is usually accompanied by a wraparound piece of cloth on the lower body and long white socks.
