Singapore


Singapore, officially the Republic of Singapore, is an island country and city-state in Southeast Asia. The country's territory comprises one main island, 63 satellite islands and islets, and one outlying islet.

Weather

Due to Singapore's tropical climate, the temperature in Singapore remains relatively stable throughout the year. Even at night time, the temperature rarely dips below 24°C. The coolest month, typically, is January, however that's only by about 1-2°C and the average highs are still around 30°C, hardly chilly

Economy


The main sectors of Singapore's economy are industry and the service sector. According to the Department of Statistics of Singapore, over 70% of nominal value added in 2023 was generated by the services industries, while about 25% was generated by the goods producing industries.

Ethnic Groups

Singaporeans with Chinese ancestry make up 75.9%, Malays make up 15.4%, Indians make up 7.4%, and residents of other descent make up 1.6% of the 3,498,200 of the resident population (excluding persons holding Permanent Residency).

There are four official languages in Singapore: English, Malay, Mandarin and Tamil. Malay is the symbolic national language, while English is the main working language.[9] Education in Singapore is bilingual, with English being the medium of instruction.

Famous Festival

Hari Raya is really famous in Singapore and seems to be the local version of the famous festival of Eid that one celebrates post Ramdaan that is a month long of fasting that happens after a month-long fasting of Ramadaan. This festival is really famous and traditional festivals that one can celebrate in Singapore.

Traditional clothing

Baju Kurung is a traditional costume as well as the national dress of Singapore and Malaysia. This traditional dress became fashionable in the late 19th century by Sultan Abu Bakar of Johar. Baju Kurung is a generic name given to the outfit for both male and female.

The male costume is recognised as Baju Melayu and the female version as Baju Kurung. The Baju Kurung has two distinct fashion methodology, the Baju Kurung Teluk Belanga and the Baju Kurung Cekak Musak.
